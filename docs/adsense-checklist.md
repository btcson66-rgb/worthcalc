# Google AdSense Approval Checklist

Use this checklist before applying for Google AdSense or before re-applying after a rejection. The goal is to show Google that the site is useful, complete, trustworthy, and safe for visitors.

## Site Content Requirements

- Publish original, useful tools that solve a real problem for visitors.
- Make every important page substantial. A page should have at least 600+ words of useful content or a working functional tool with supporting explanations.
- Remove thin, empty, duplicated, or near-duplicate pages before applying.
- Do not copy tool descriptions, FAQ answers, examples, or blog content from another site.
- Give each site a clear unique value proposition. If multiple sites use the same tool template, rewrite the content and positioning for each audience.
- Avoid placeholder pages, "coming soon" pages, test pages, and pages that look unfinished.
- Check that every page a visitor can open has a clear purpose.

## Required Pages

- About: explain who runs the site, what the site offers, and why the tools exist.
- Privacy Policy: disclose GA4, AdSense cookies, analytics tracking, advertising cookies, and basic data practices.
- Terms of Service: explain acceptable use, limitations, and user responsibility.
- Contact: provide a real email address that someone can use for support or policy questions.
- Disclaimer: explain that tools are provided for convenience and are not professional legal, financial, medical, or technical advice unless reviewed by a qualified expert.

## Ad Placement Rules

- Ads must not cover, block, or obstruct tool functionality.
- Do not use pop-up ads or overlays that interrupt visitors.
- Do not place ads where they can be mistaken for buttons, inputs, download links, navigation, or tool results.
- Use no more than 3 ad units per page.
- Keep ads clearly labeled, for example with "Advertisement" near the ad slot.
- Leave enough spacing between interactive controls and ads so visitors do not click ads by accident.
- Never ask users to click ads or imply that ads are part of the tool workflow.

## Navigation & UX

- Provide working navigation on every page.
- Make legal pages accessible from the footer.
- Make the site mobile responsive and readable on small screens.
- Keep pages fast. Aim for load time under 3 seconds on mobile.
- Fix all broken internal links and important external links.
- Use SSL/HTTPS for the full site.
- Make sure the header, footer, tool area, FAQ, and related links are easy to find.

## Tool Quality

- Each tool must actually work.
- Tools should provide real value and produce clear, understandable results.
- Handle edge cases gracefully, such as empty input, invalid input, very long input, or unsupported files.
- Do not add fake "processing" delays.
- Do not show fake output, fake progress, or misleading claims.
- Explain any limits clearly, such as browser-only processing, maximum file size, or approximate calculations.
- Test tools on desktop and mobile before applying.

## Common Rejection Reasons

- Thin content with little original explanation.
- No original content or copied descriptions from other sites.
- Too many ads or ads that make the page hard to use.
- Broken pages, broken navigation, or tools that do not work.
- Missing legal pages.
- Copied images, icons, templates, or articles without permission.
- Under construction pages or placeholder text.
- Pages that are indexed but not ready for visitors.

## Re-application

- Wait 2-4 weeks before re-applying after a rejection.
- Fix all issues, not just the one you think caused the rejection.
- Review content quality, legal pages, navigation, mobile layout, broken links, and ad placement again.
- Do not re-apply immediately without making meaningful changes.
- Keep notes on what was fixed so the next review is easier.
